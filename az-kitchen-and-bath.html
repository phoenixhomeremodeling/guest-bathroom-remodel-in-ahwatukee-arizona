Frequently Asked Questions

Is DIY bathroom remodel worth it?

In summary, a bathroom remodel will typically cost less than half of what a general contractor (GC) would charge, as it allows you to keep the material costs while saving on labor expenses, but the decision to hire a GC may be justified for the convenience it offers, which can be considered a luxury worth the additional cost.

What is the least expensive way to install a shower?

To create an affordable shower, opt for a straightforward design utilizing cost-effective materials, such as pre-fabricated shower kits, acrylic or fiberglass walls, basic plumbing fixtures, a shower curtain instead of an enclosure, and standard tiles or a basic shower base.

What is the most expensive part of a bathroom?

In a bathroom renovation, the wet area, which includes the bathtub, shower, or a combination of both and the surrounding walls, is typically the most costly segment due to waterproofing requirements, as it's designed to withstand water exposure.

Why remodeling a bathroom is expensive?

Bathroom remodeling is typically one of the most expensive home projects, as it often entails skilled labor, plumbing and electrical work, the use of high-end materials and fixtures, and potential updates to the room's layout.

How do I budget for a bathroom renovation?

For standard guidelines, you can consult the National Kitchen and Bath Association's cost breakdown, which includes percentages for various aspects of a remodel such as design (4%), labor (20%), flooring (9%), walls & ceilings (5%), doors & windows (4%), faucets & plumbing (14%), countertops (7%), and cabinetry & hardware (16%).
